DeepTex is works especially well on objects with glossy surfaces, as it is able to completely remove specular highlights from the resulting texture. In comparison, specular highlights end up in the textures produced by all other tested texturing approaches.

Abstract

Image-based 3D reconstruction is a commonly used technique for measuring the geometry and color of objects or scenes based on images. While the geometry reconstruction of state-of-the-art approaches is mostly robust against varying lighting conditions and outliers, these pose a significant challenge for calculating an accurate texture map. This work proposes a deep-learning based texturing approach called “DeepTex” that uses a custom learned blending method on top of a traditional mosaic-based texturing approach. The model was trained using a custom synthetic data generation workflow and showed a significantly increased accuracy when generating textures in the presence of outliers and non-uniform lighting.

Network Design

Overview of the proposed network structure. The input volume is processed using three consecutive 3D convolution blocks (3D Conv), each followed by a ReLU activation and a MaxPooling operation (MaxPool). Afterwards two fully connected layers (FC) are used to produce a weight vector that is multiplied with the 15 candidate colors of the center pixel to produce and output color.

Qualitative Results

In our tests, DeepTex is the only texturing approach that is able to completely remove all shading information from the input texture, yielding a high quality albedo map.
